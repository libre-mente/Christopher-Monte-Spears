The Journey

Music has always been more than a discipline; it is the language I grew up speaking. My earliest memories are wrapped in the sound of the guitar, watching my dad play. He was a guitarist, and he shaped the way I look at the instrument.

When I was 12 years old, he handed me my very first guitar and taught me how to play. But what he really taught me was how to communicate without speaking. Growing up, him and I would have entire conversations with our guitars where we would never even say a single word. The message was perfectly conveyed in the music we shared together. That profound connection sparked a lifelong love and passion for the craft.

Today, that raw emotional foundation drives my creative pursuit, pushing me to fuse the technical precision of classical training with the unvarnished, storytelling power of poetry.
